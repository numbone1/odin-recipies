Boiled Bagels

Description

Boiled bagels are a delightful culinary adventure waiting to be explored by new cooks. These chewy, golden-brown treats boast a crisp exterior and a soft, doughy interior that's nothing short of a taste revelation. The process of boiling the bagels before baking sets them apart, giving them that iconic texture and authentic taste. It's a simple yet rewarding technique that involves boiling the bagels in water mixed with a touch of honey or malt syrup before they hit the oven. The boiling not only adds a glossy finish but also helps in achieving that perfect chewiness we all love. So, whether you're a newbie in the kitchen or an aspiring baker, trying your hand at making boiled bagels can be a fun and delicious start to your culinary journey.

Embarking on the journey to make boiled bagels is an exciting way to explore the art of baking while enjoying the rich flavors and textures that this iconic breakfast item offers. With just a few basic ingredients like flour, water, yeast, and a pinch of sugar, you can create a dough that will transform into the delicious bagel base. The magic truly happens when you dip these doughy rings into a simmering pot of water, allowing them to puff up and absorb the sweet infusion of honey or malt syrup. The boiling process not only enhances the bagel's taste and appearance but also gives you the perfect canvas to experiment with various toppings and flavors. So, roll up your sleeves, embrace the dough, and experience the joy of creating your own homemade boiled bagels - a delectable treat that will leave your taste buds yearning for more.

  • 2 (.25 ounce) packages active dry yeast
  • 4 tablespoons white sugar, divided
  • 1 tablespoon salt

Steps

  1. Combine 1 1/2 cups flour and yeast in a large bowl. Mix water, 3 tablespoons sugar, and salt together; add to dry ingredients. Beat with a mixer for half a minute at low speed, scraping the sides of the bowl clean. Beat at a higher speed for 3 minutes. Then mix in enough flour by hand to make a moderately stiff dough.
  2. Turn out onto a lightly floured surface and knead until smooth and elastic, 8 to 10 minutes. Cover and let rest for 15 minutes.
  3. Cut into 12 portions and shape into smooth balls. Poke a hole in the center with your finger, and gently enlarge the hole while working the bagel into a uniform shape. Cover and let rise for 20 minutes.
  4. Meanwhile, start a gallon of water boiling. Add in remaining 1 tablespoon sugar and mix it around a bit. Reduce to simmering.
  5. Preheat the oven to 375 degrees F (190 degrees C).
  6. When bagels have risen, put 4 or 5 bagels into the water and cook for 7 minutes, turning once. Drain.
  7. Place drained bagels on a greased baking sheet and bake in the preheated oven for 30 to 35 minutes. Remove from the oven and eat hot or cold.
